New York gas prices in comparison to surrounding states

By Gustavo Garcia

April 26, 2022

View from the Interstate 278 overpass at the foot of the The Robert F. Kennedy Bridge that connects the New York City boroughs of Queens, Manhattan, and the Bronx. (Photo by Gustavo Garcia)

Gasoline prices continue to be high because of the oil market volatility. Across the board the cost of gas per gallon is around 4 dollars in the Mid-Atlantic region and parts of New England. This is according to data by AAA.

In New York the current average gas price is $4.24 per gallon. The price continues to edge down from its highest recorded average price of $4.47 for regular unleaded gas that was reached on March 10, 2022.

As the weather continues to get warmer and people begin to plan trips to the surrounding areas you can calculate how much you could pay at the pump in nearby states using the gas price calculator.
